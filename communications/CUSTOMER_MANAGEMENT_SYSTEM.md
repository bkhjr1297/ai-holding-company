# Customer Management System

## Operating Promise

Revenue Rescue Desk should feel like an established operations partner from the first interaction.

We do that through:

- clear onboarding
- written success criteria
- weekly reporting
- documented commitments
- consistent follow-up
- escalation rules
- client health tracking

## Customer Lifecycle

1. Lead created
2. Discovery booked
3. Audit completed
4. Pilot proposed
5. Pilot active
6. Weekly report delivered
7. Conversion to monthly retainer
8. Monthly optimization review
9. Renewal / expansion

## Client Onboarding Checklist

- Confirm services offered
- Confirm service areas
- Confirm best contact for urgent handoffs
- Confirm calendar/dispatch workflow
- Confirm business hours and after-hours rules
- Confirm lead qualification questions
- Confirm tone and banned claims
- Confirm escalation rules
- Confirm weekly report day

## Weekly Report Structure

```text
Subject: Weekly recovered-opportunity report — [Client]

Hi [Name],

Here is this week's response-desk summary:

- New missed opportunities processed: [#]
- Customers contacted: [#]
- Qualified opportunities: [#]
- Appointments booked / handed off: [#]
- No response / bad fit: [#]
- Average response time: [time]
- Estimated recovered revenue: [$]

Observations:
- [Insight 1]
- [Insight 2]

Recommended next improvement:
- [Action]

Best,
Revenue Rescue Desk
```

## Client Health Scoring

| Health | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Green | weekly value shown, responsive client, appointments booked |
| Yellow | low volume, slow client replies, unclear handoff process |
| Red | no access to opportunities, client not responding, unresolved complaints |

## Retention Rule

Every client must see a tangible weekly artifact: a report, call log, booked appointment list, or process improvement. No silent fulfillment.

# Fulfillment SOP

## Service Promise

Recover missed calls/web inquiries and convert qualified opportunities into booked appointments or warm handoffs.

## Inputs Needed From Client

- Business name and service area
- Services offered
- Phone number(s)
- Calendar/booking process
- Dispatch/contact person
- Qualification rules
- Emergency service rules
- Pricing/estimate boundaries
- Preferred follow-up tone
- Access to missed-call list, voicemail notifications, contact forms, or manual exports

## Daily Workflow

1. Pull new missed opportunities.
2. Log each opportunity in CRM/tracker.
3. Classify source: missed call, voicemail, contact form, chat, email.
4. Follow up using approved script/channel.
5. Qualify: service needed, location, urgency, decision maker, availability.
6. Book appointment or hand off to client.
7. Record outcome.
8. Queue follow-up if no response.
9. Update weekly report.

## Opportunity Statuses

- New
- Attempted 1
- Attempted 2
- Attempted 3
- Contacted
- Qualified
- Booked
- Not a Fit
- Lost / No Response
- Needs Client Input

## QA Checklist

- Was the opportunity logged?
- Was follow-up attempted within target SLA?
- Was tone professional and client-approved?
- Was the outcome recorded?
- Was next action/date set?
- Was appointment information complete?
- Did anything require client escalation?

## Weekly Client Report

Include:

- Total opportunities processed
- Contacted
- Qualified
- Booked
- No response
- Estimated recovered revenue
- Fastest/average response time
- Lead leakage observations
- Recommended process improvements

## Escalation Rules

Escalate to client immediately when:

- Customer is angry or urgent.
- Job requires emergency dispatch.
- Pricing/scope question exceeds approved script.
- Existing customer has complaint.
- Legal/compliance issue appears.
- Appointment availability is unclear.

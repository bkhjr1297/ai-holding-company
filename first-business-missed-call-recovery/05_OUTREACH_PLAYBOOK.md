# Outreach Playbook

## ICP

Home-service companies with urgent inbound demand and high-value jobs.

Initial target filters:

- 5-50 employees or owner-operated with trucks/crews.
- Has phone number and website/contact form.
- Offers emergency or high-ticket service.
- Has reviews indicating missed calls, scheduling delays, or slow response.
- Runs ads or has SEO presence, meaning missed leads are costly.

## Lead Sources — $0

1. Google Maps manual searches.
2. Yelp / BBB / Angi listings.
3. Local chamber directories.
4. Contractor association member lists.
5. Websites with "request quote" forms.
6. Review mining for complaints about slow response or scheduling.
7. Facebook/local groups for service recommendations.

## Qualification Checklist

| Signal | Good Fit? |
|---|---|
| Phone visible on site/listing | yes |
| Contact form present | yes |
| Emergency/high-value service | yes |
| Reviews mention scheduling/response delays | high priority |
| Owner/manager identifiable | high priority |
| Multiple service areas or crews | high priority |
| Already running ads | high priority |

## Cold Call Script

```text
Hi, is this [Company]?

My name is [Name] with Revenue Rescue Desk. We help local service companies recover missed calls and quote requests so more jobs actually make it onto the calendar.

Quick question — when your team misses a call or a web quote request comes in after hours, do you have a consistent follow-up process, or is it mostly handled when someone has time?
```

If interested:

```text
That makes sense. What we usually find is that even a few missed or slow-followed-up inquiries can equal thousands in lost jobs each month.

We run a simple response desk: track missed opportunities, follow up quickly, qualify the customer, and help book the appointment — then send you a weekly report showing what was recovered.

Would it be worth a 15-minute call to see if there are easy wins in your current follow-up process?
```

If skeptical:

```text
Totally fair. Most owners already have someone answering phones. The gap is usually overflow, after-hours, and quote requests that don't get followed up fast enough.

Would it be unreasonable to do a quick missed-revenue audit and show you where leads might be leaking before you decide anything?
```

Voicemail:

```text
Hi [Name], this is [Name] with Revenue Rescue Desk. We help home-service companies recover missed calls and quote requests so more jobs get booked. I had one quick idea for [Company]'s follow-up process. I'll send a short email as well — again, [Name] at Revenue Rescue Desk, [phone].
```

## Cold Email 1

Subject: `missed calls at [Company]?`

```text
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] handles [service] in [city]. For home-service businesses, even a few missed calls or slow quote follow-ups can turn into lost jobs — especially after hours or during busy days.

We run a lightweight response desk that follows up with missed calls/web inquiries, qualifies the customer, helps book the appointment, and sends a weekly recovered-opportunity report.

Would it be worth a 15-minute call to see if there are any obvious lead leaks at [Company]?

[Name]
Revenue Rescue Desk
```

## Follow-Up 1

Subject: `quick idea for [Company]`

```text
Hi [First Name],

One practical place we look first: calls that go to voicemail, quote forms submitted after 5pm, and leads that sit more than 15-30 minutes before response.

If [Company] already has that covered, no worries. If not, we can usually set up a simple follow-up workflow without changing your phone system.

Open to a quick missed-revenue audit this week?
```

## Breakup Email

Subject: `close the loop?`

```text
Hi [First Name],

I haven't heard back, so I'll close the loop for now.

If missed calls, quote requests, or after-hours follow-up ever become a priority, we're happy to run a simple audit and show what might be leaking.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Discovery Call Agenda

1. How many inbound calls/quote requests do you get weekly?
2. What happens when no one answers?
3. Who follows up on voicemail/web forms?
4. How fast does follow-up usually happen?
5. What is an average booked job worth?
6. What calendar/dispatch system do you use?
7. What would one extra booked job per week be worth?
8. If we could track and recover missed opportunities, would a monthly response desk make sense?

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| We already answer our phones | Great — this is mainly about overflow, after-hours, and leads that don't get followed up fast enough. |
| We don't need AI | Totally fair. The outcome is not AI; it's more booked jobs and fewer missed opportunities. |
| Too expensive | If one recovered job pays for the month, would it be worth testing with a small pilot? |
| Send me info | Happy to. What should I focus on — missed calls, web leads, or after-hours follow-up? |
| Not interested | Understood. If you ever want a no-pressure missed-revenue audit, I'm happy to help. |

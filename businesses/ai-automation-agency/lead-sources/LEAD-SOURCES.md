# Lead Sources

## Tier 1 — High intent, immediate
1. **Cold outreach to frustrated operators.** Target SMB owners who post about missed calls or backlogged support in public communities, then reach out with a specific automation idea.
2. **Referral partnerships.** Transaction coordinators, bookkeepers, VAs, and fractional COOs who already see the operational pain.
3. **Outbound form-fill audits.** Check a prospect's website form submission behavior; if auto-reply is generic or missing, pitch lead response automation with a recorded audit snippet.

## Tier 2 — Compounding content
4. **Before/after timelapse clips.** Show a 30–60 second side-by-side of "manual triage" vs. "automated triage," with time saved and SLA met.
5. **Short case studies.** Publish 3-paragraph outcomes: problem, automation built, measurable result.
6. **LinkedIn and local groups.** Post operational automation insights weekly. CTA: "Reply BUILD for a free workflow audit."

## Tier 3 — Partnerships and marketplaces
7. **Agency referrals.** Partner with marketing agencies that need fulfillment support for automation-heavy clients.
8. **Tool communities.** Be active in Zapier/Make/n8n communities; answer implementation questions with authority.
9. **Local chambers and B2B meetups.** Offer a 20-minute talk on "3 automations that save 10+ hours per week."

## Tier 4 — Paid channel (only after organic proven)
10. **LinkedIn lead ads** targeting ops managers and founders in target industries.
11. **YouTube short-form demos.** "Watch me replace this manual process with one Zap + one AI rule."
12. **Google Search Ads.** Keywords: "AI automation for small business," "workflow automation agency," "lead response automation setup."

## Daily activity commitment
- 10 outbound operator conversations per day
- 1 short content piece per day or 3 longer pieces per week
- 1 referral conversation per day
- Weekly review of lead conversion rates; cut channels under 3% response

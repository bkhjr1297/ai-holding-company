## Partner Strategy

### Ideal referral partners

- Podcast equipment retailers (Rode, Shure)
- Podcasting accelerators / MFA programs
- Creator economy funds
- PR agencies that overflow client audio needs
- VC-backed startups looking to "build a show" as lead gen

### Referral terms

- 10% of first month's recurring revenue (not setup fee).
- Unique tracking link + UTM codes per partner.
- Quarterly settlement.

## SEO Playbook

**Target intent clusters:**

1. "podcast editing service" → comparison + benchmarks
2. "how much does podcast editing cost" → detailed cost-per-minute analysis
3. "podcast production company" → agency credentials + process
4. "Libsyn vs Buzzsprout for RSS" — informational bridge content
5. "how to start a podcast (for business)" → educational lead magnet

**Landing pages to build:**
- `/services`
- `/pricing`
- `/about`
- `/case-study-{client}`

**On-page priorities:**
- H1 + H2 keyword match.
- Schema.org `PodcastSeries` markup.
- RSS URL in JSON-LD (if client permits).

## Cold Outreach Sequence (3-Email)

**Audience:** Showname with >5K subs, >60-day gap, strong brand alignment.

**Email 1:**
Subject: Gap analysis for "{Show}" — quick take

Body:
> Noticed you've been on a 90-day publishing break. The format that got you to 12K subscribers — solo, 35 min, practical — still works, but the production line takes it out of you. We run post-production on 3 similar shows: editing + show notes + dir submit within 48 hrs. If you're thinking about coming back, happy to send a complimentary episode analysis.

**Email 2 (48 hrs later):**
Subject: 48hr turnaround (for your next episode)

Body:
> Quick follow-up. If you're still considering options, our Ignite plan covers 4 episodes/mo at $1,499. We handle chapters, distribution, and show notes so you can focus on recording. Would make your comeback easier.

**Email 3 (96 hrs later):**
Subject: Credible option to unblock your schedule

Body:
> Last one from me for a bit. We work with 4 similar tech-side shows. One went from 2 episodes/mo to weekly without changing hosts' workload. If you want a no-pressure intro, here's our process sheet.

## Community + Content Strategy

**Reddit / Podcasting threads:**
- Do not DM cold with pricing. Comment with specific advice first.
- Share: "I audited a show recently and found X, Y, Z production issues." Build street cred.

**LinkedIn:**
- Publish 1 article/week: production process, distribution checklist.
- Tag clients (with permission) for social proof.

**Twitter/X:**
- Thread: "50 tips for indie podcasters — the 17 I'd pay for."
- Clip our clients' episodes (with permission) to show range.
